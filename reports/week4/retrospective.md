# Sprint Retrospective — Week 4

## What went well

1. **Application demo was ready for the customer review.** The team managed to deliver a working prototype with core features — receipt scanning, inventory management, and recipe suggestions — in time for the Sprint Review session.

2. **Clear role distribution improved delivery speed.** Backend engineers focused on API and database work while the UI/UX designer handled interface components. This separation reduced blockers and allowed parallel progress.

3. **Customer communication remained open and constructive.** The customer provided detailed and actionable feedback during the Sprint Review, which gave the team a clear direction for the next Sprint. The team handled the feedback professionally.

## What did not go well

1. **Too many placeholder screens in the demo.** Several parts of the application shown to the customer were stubs rather than functional implementations, particularly the daily meal planner. This reduced confidence in the overall delivery.

2. **Barcode scanning broke during the demo.** A feature that previously worked stopped functioning during the live Sprint Review session. This highlighted a lack of pre-demo testing and smoke-check verification.

3. **Uneven workload distribution across the team.** The majority of technical work was concentrated on one or two team members, while others had significantly fewer Story Points assigned. This created bottlenecks and risk.

## What we changed compared to the previous Sprint

Based on the Sprint 1 retrospective, we attempted to improve task distribution by assigning Story Points more evenly across team members and making assignees explicit in GitHub Projects. This was partially successful but still needs improvement.

## Action points for the next Sprint

1. **Run a smoke check before every customer demo** — assign one team member to verify all key features work end-to-end at least one hour before the meeting starts.
2. **Distribute Sprint tasks more evenly** — no single team member should carry more than 40% of the total Sprint Story Points.
